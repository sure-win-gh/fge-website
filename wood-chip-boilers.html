Froling Technology

Intelligent design,
engineered to last

All components in a Froling wood chip boiler are designed to maximise efficiency, reduce emissions and minimise energy consumption — targeting convenience, safety and reliability across the full output range.

Silicon Carbide Combustion Chamber

The T4e's silicon carbide combustion chamber achieves up to 96.3% efficiency with very low emissions. Its exceptional heat resistance ensures long service life, while the optimised geometry promotes complete combustion across all fuel types and load conditions.

Lambdatronic 3200 Controller

Froling's advanced combustion management system continuously monitors and adjusts air supply, fuel feed and temperatures to maintain peak efficiency. Operational data is logged automatically, service reminders are built in, and the intuitive interface puts full control at your fingertips.

Energy-Saving EC Drive Motors

Well-planned use of energy-saving EC (electronically commutated) drives for all motors and actuators ensures extremely low electrical energy consumption during operation — keeping running costs down and underpinning the Froling EnergieGenie award for the T4e.

Automatic Ash Removal

The automated ash removal system transfers ash from the combustion chamber to a collection drawer with minimal user intervention. Depending on usage intensity, emptying is typically only required every few weeks — making day-to-day operation as straightforward as any conventional heating system.

Modular Construction

Froling wood chip boilers are built with modular components for ease of transport and installation — particularly valuable in agricultural and industrial settings where access can be restricted. The T4e ships fully assembled, electrically wired and tested, minimising on-site commissioning time.

Froling Connect — Remote Monitoring

Monitor and control your Froling wood chip boiler remotely via the Froling Connect platform. View live efficiency data, receive fault alerts, adjust operating schedules and track service intervals from anywhere — ideal for multi-site operators or remote agricultural installations.

Why Go Wood Chip

The case for wood chip
biomass heating

Wood chip is carbon neutral, locally produced and significantly cheaper than fossil fuels — making it a compelling choice for agricultural, commercial and industrial sites looking to cut costs and carbon simultaneously.

Carbon-Neutral, Locally Sourced Fuel

Wood chip is classified as a renewable, carbon-neutral fuel — the CO₂ released during combustion is offset by the carbon absorbed as the trees grew. Production also supports local employment and keeps supply chains short. Switching from oil to wood chip can cut heating carbon emissions by over 90%.

Significantly Lower Running Costs

Wood chip fuel costs considerably less per kWh than heating oil, mains gas or LPG. Because it is produced locally and not traded on global commodity markets, prices are far more stable and predictable — giving businesses and farms far greater control over their energy costs year-on-year.

Wide Fuel Flexibility

Most Froling wood chip boilers can also burn wood pellets and shavings, giving you the flexibility to switch fuel sources depending on local availability and price. This multi-fuel capability provides a genuine hedge against supply disruption and price volatility.

Commercial & Agricultural Scale Output

From a 20kW system for a smaller agricultural building to a 1,500kW Lambdamat installation heating a large industrial facility or district heating network, Froling's wood chip range scales to meet real-world demand. All systems are designed to operate reliably at full output in demanding environments.

Common Questions

Wood chip boiler
FAQs

What is a Froling wood chip boiler?

A Froling wood chip boiler is a fully automatic biomass heating system that burns locally sourced wood chip — and in most models, pellets and shavings too — to generate heat for agricultural, commercial and industrial buildings. Models range from 20kW to 1,500kW, all featuring Froling's intelligent combustion control and energy-saving drive systems.

What fuel types can a Froling wood chip boiler burn?

Most Froling wood chip boilers are multi-fuel capable. The T4e burns wood chip, pellets and shavings. The Turbomat handles chip and pellets. The TMe and Lambdamat burn chip, shavings and pellets — with the Lambdamat also accepting wet fuels up to 50% moisture content. This flexibility lets you switch between fuel sources to take advantage of local supply and pricing.

What size wood chip boiler do I need?

Sizing depends on your building's heat demand, insulation and the area you need to heat. The T4e at 20–350kW suits most agricultural and smaller commercial buildings. Larger commercial or industrial sites may require the Turbomat (150–550kW) or TMe (400–550kW). For high-output district heating or industrial process heat, the Lambdamat (750–1,500kW) is Fröling's flagship large-scale solution. Our technical team provides free heat loss assessments and system sizing.

How is wood chip fuel stored and fed to the boiler?

Wood chip is stored in a dedicated fuel store or silo adjacent to the boiler room. Froling offers automated auger and outfeed systems to convey chip from the store to the boiler automatically — the same hands-off operation you'd expect from an oil or gas system. Store size is designed to match your consumption pattern.

Are wood chip boilers eligible for the BUS grant?

Biomass boilers — including wood chip models — may be eligible for the UK Government's Boiler Upgrade Scheme (BUS), providing a £5,000 grant for eligible domestic properties in England and Wales. Eligibility depends on property type, current heating system and MCS installer certification. Contact Freedom Green for a free eligibility check.

How often does a wood chip boiler need servicing?

An annual service by a qualified HETAS engineer is recommended. Froling's Lambdatronic control system provides built-in service reminders and logs all operational data, making maintenance straightforward. Freedom Green connects you with trained Froling service engineers across the UK.
